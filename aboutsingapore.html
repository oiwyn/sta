About Singapore

Brief Introduction

Singapore or officially called 'Republic of Singapore' is a small country stand on 716.1 km² land area with the population total of 5.399.200. The citizen who lives in Singapore is called Singaporean. The national language is English, but there are also some languages that Singaporeans use like Malay, Mandarin, and Tamil. Although English is the most used and its official language in Singapore, the national anthem ‘Majulah Singapura’ is actually sung in Malay. Despite being urbanized with the water area total of only 1.44%, Singapore is the largest exporter of ornamental fish (25% of the world market).

The national flag of Singapore uses red symbol which mean universal brotherhood and equality of man while white signifies purity and virtue. The crescent moon represents a young nation on the rise and the five stars signify the ideals of democracy, peace, progress, justice and equality. Singapore country is located at UTC +8 time zone with the date format of dd/mm/yyyy. You will need +65 telephone code to call the people who live in Singapore. And lastly Singapore uses Singapore Dollar ($) as its currency.

Fun Facts

  • The world’s highest man-made waterfall, standing at 30 metres, is located at the Jurong BirdPark.
  • 8 in 10 people in Singapore own cell phones. In fact, telecom companies issue new numbers at the rate of 30,000 to 40,000 per month.
  • Singapore is a stopover point for thousands of migratory birds travelling the East Asian Flyway.
  • Singlish is a unique blend of English, Chinese, Malay, Tamil and local dialects.
